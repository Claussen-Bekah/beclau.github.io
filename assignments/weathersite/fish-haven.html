Barn Construction Tips

By Hailey Cedar

I have some great tips for those of you who want to be the envy of your neighbors (and your neighbors' livestock!) and build the barn of your wildest dreams. First, let's talk materials. The best barns have a stone or cement base, with wood framing and some sort of waterproof exterior. I recommend metal roofs so that the snow slides off easily, but if your barn's roof pitch is steep enough, you can get away with shingles, which look better.

Next, you'll want to think about how you want your barn to look, and it's dimensions. Dimensions are important, and largely depends on how much space you need (duh!). Think about your livestock and your storage needs and then go talk to Richard Henderson. He knows a thing or two about barns and would be happy to help you draw up some plans.

Painting your barn is obviously for aesthetic reasons. I love a good red barn, but feel free to branch out and get blue or green or even just stick to white!
